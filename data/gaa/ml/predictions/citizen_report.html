üî¨ How We Detected Anomalies

Our system uses two powerful detection methods to find unusual budget patterns:

ü§ñ 1. Machine Learning (Isolation Forest)

An AI algorithm that learns what "normal" budgets look like across all departments, then identifies items that significantly deviate from these patterns.

üìä 2. Statistical Analysis (Z-Score)

Mathematical analysis that calculates how far each budget item is from the typical average. Items more than 3 standard deviations away are flagged.

Important: An anomaly doesn't mean wrongdoing - it could indicate new programs, emergency responses, or policy changes that deserve public attention.

üìà Spending Pattern Groups

üî¨ How We Grouped Agencies

We used a machine learning technique called K-means Clustering to group government agencies based on their spending behavior. The analysis considered:

  • Total Budget Size: How much each agency typically receives
  • Growth Patterns: Whether their budgets tend to increase, decrease, or stay stable
  • Budget Share: What percentage of the total government budget they receive
  • Spending Stability: Whether their allocations are consistent or vary significantly

Why group agencies? Grouping helps identify agencies with similar spending behavior, making it easier to compare efficiency, detect unusual patterns, and understand government priorities.
